# Montparnasse

**A literary biopic of Kiki de Montparnasse — narrative nonfiction with disclosed composite characters.**

A recast of Richard Triberg's 220-page Exposé *"The Extraordinary Life & Times of the Immortal yet Forgotten KIKI, Queen of Montparnasse"* into a twelve-chapter literary biography, told in parallel with Triberg's four-year dig to authenticate Modigliani's *Sitting Nude with Crossed Hands* (c. 1917) at Institut Restellini, Geneva.

## The book in one paragraph

Two lives running side by side, separated by exactly one hundred years. Kiki Prin — born Châtillon-sur-Seine 1901, the Madonna of Montparnasse, the most photographed woman in Paris, dead alone in a hospital ward in 1953 at fifty-one. And Richard Triberg, who spent the last four years putting her story back together from the documents the world had buried, with one small canvas — Modigliani's *Sitting Nude with Crossed Hands* — at Institut Restellini in Geneva right now, waiting on a signature. The book is the four years. It begins with a phone call. It ends with a phone call.
