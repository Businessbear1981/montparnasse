# Kiki — Master Timeline

*Locked 30 May 2026. Every Lost Boy's position, year by year, 1901–1953. Built so subsequent chapters braid the cast through continuously instead of treating each as a cameo. Drawn from Mark Braude, Kiki's 1929 memoir, Richard's Exposé, the catalogues raisonnés. Where dates are approximate, flagged.*

---

## How to use this

When drafting chapters VI, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII: open this document. Find the year the chapter slips into. Look at who is in her room that year. **Bring at least three of those figures into the chapter — at minimum a recurring named appearance even if just a glance.** A character the reader does not see at least twice before dying does not have a death the reader feels.

## The year-by-year

### 1901–1913 — Châtillon

Born 2 Sept 1901. Raised by grandmother in Châtillon-sur-Seine. Father never claimed her. Mother in Paris as a *linotypiste.* Sent to Paris age 12 in 1913.

### 1914 — Paris, age 13

Apprentice in a bakery and then a bookbinder. Begins drawing portraits at the bar.

### 1915 — age 14

Begins modelling for sculptors after being slapped/fired from the bakery for resisting an unwanted advance. Her mother crashes the studio, calls her *"miserable little whore,"* disowns her. **Chez Rosalie's** as a waitress. Meets **Modi** there — he growls at her, makes her shiver, scares her. *(her memoir, direct quote)*

### 1916 — age 15

Continues at Chez Rosalie's. Modi sketches her obsessively on backs of menus / cigarette boxes through 1916–1917 — *not formal sittings.* He pays for none of it. She is paid by the sculptor and a few other painters who use her as a nude model.

### 1917 — age 16 (Painting Year)

- **Spring–summer:** Modi shares the rue de la Grande Chaumière studio with **C.R.W. Nevinson.** Sisley Huddleston sits in the corner taking notes for an American magazine. The shared studio. The red velvet pillow. The white shawl.
- **11 October 1917:** Alice gives the apple to **Aaron** (Bill's great-uncle), tubercular colour merchant's apprentice, at the corner of the rue du Montparnasse and the rue Vavin. The single sentence Aaron will write down in 1968. *Richard's parcel from Bill.*
- **Autumn 1917:** Modi paints **Sitting Nude with Crossed Hands.** Our painting.
- **December 1917:** Berthe Weill mounts the Modigliani Nudes show. Closed by Paris police on opening day for indecency. Sitting Nude with Crossed Hands is on the wall.

### 1918 — age 17

- January: Modi marries Jeanne Hébuterne unofficially.
- Spring: Spanish flu hits. Kiki gets it. Survives.
- Summer: meets **Mendjizky.** Polish painter, 28. He pays her to sit.

### 1919 — age 18

- Living off-and-on with Mendjizky in his small studio on the rue Joseph-Bara.
- Mendjizky paints her six surviving oils between now and 1922.
- **Names her *Kiki*** — from Yiddish *malenkaya kiki,* "little one," what his mother's Bratislava cousins said. The name will stick.
- Modi is failing. Coughs blood daily. The bottle of cheap brandy. The Coquillé lead-white on credit.

### 1920 — age 18 (rolling over to 19 in October)

- **24 January 1920:** Modi dies, tubercular meningitis, Hôpital de la Charité.
- **25 January 1920:** Jeanne jumps from a fifth-floor window on the rue Amyot. Eight months pregnant. Modi has been dead about eight hours.
- Alice is 18. Reads about Modi in a paper. Sits on the step of his empty studio on the rue de la Grande Chaumière in the late afternoons watching the chimney-pot shadows cross the gutter. Cannot afford to mourn formally. *(Sean's Chapter V slip.)*
- Sleeping in Thérèse Treize de Caro's room when she does not sleep at Mendjizky's.

### 1921 — age 19–20

- **July 1921: Man Ray** arrives in Paris from New York. Within weeks meets Kiki at La Rotonde where the *patron* refuses her a table. Man Ray vouches.
- Late 1921: Mendjizky relationship ends; **Man Ray** begins. The two men know each other and remain in the same cafés.
- **Autumn 1921:** the Jockey 1921 photograph is taken. Kiki at the centre, Man Ray front row, Pound middle row, Cocteau & Tzara front, *Little Review* editors flanking her. **Bill Bird, Holger Cahill, Hilaire Hiler, Curtiss Moffitt** in the back row.
- Soutine is in her orbit. Foujita is in her orbit. Pascin is in her orbit.

### 1922 — age 20–21

- Living with Man Ray at **31 bis rue Campagne-Première.** She cooks (Braude: Burgundian dishes, stretched their money).
- Henri Broca first appears in the cafés, drawing cartoons of her.
- Mendjizky still in orbit but no longer her lover.

### 1923 — age 21–22

- Man Ray's first film **Le Retour à la Raison.** She appears in it, uncredited. Tristan Tzara organised the Dada show it premiered at. *André Breton breaks Pierre de Massot's arm at the same event. Police arrive.*
- Hemingway has been in Paris since 1921. He is now a writer for the *Toronto Star,* working on *In Our Time.*
- She is travelling to New York to try to break into film — returns without filming anything.

### 1924 — age 22–23 (peak begins)

- **November 1923 — Jockey Club opens** at 127 rue Notre-Dame-des-Champs. Hiler's cowboy murals. She begins singing there in **1924.** Braude: *"If a singing voice could smell, hers would be garlic hitting a pan's hot butter and wine."*
- **Man Ray makes *Le Violon d'Ingres.*** The most expensive photograph in the world (2022).
- Léger's *Ballet Mécanique* — her face juxtaposed with machines. Cocteau is everywhere. **Henri-Pierre Roché** sees one of her watercolours and writes in his diary it is a *"super-Matisse."* Buys ten of her paintings across the next years.
- **Bill Bird** publishes Hemingway's *in our time* on Three Mountains Press.

### 1925 — age 23–24 (room at full burn)

- **31 bis rue Campagne-Première.** The kitchen. Tuesday nights. Soutine, Foujita, Mendjizky, Pascin, Man Ray. *(Sean's Chapter VII.)*
- **October 1925:** Josephine Baker debuts La Revue Nègre at the Théâtre des Champs-Élysées. The 1920s cabaret world widens.
- Pascin at peak. His best nudes are this year.
- **Cocteau** in the Boeuf sur le Toit circle nightly. Opium.

### 1926 — age 24–25

- **Man Ray's *Noire et Blanche*** — Kiki with the African mask. *French Vogue,* May 1926.
- **Emak-Bakia,** Man Ray's second film. Kiki in it, uncredited.
- The Coupole opens (15 December 1927). She moves between Jockey + Dôme + Sélect + Coupole.

### 1927 — age 25–26 (her own peak)

- **Au Sacre du Printemps gallery, Paris — her FIRST solo painting show. All 27 pieces sold.** Good reviews. *L'Acrobate,* later a Kiki auction record.
- Pascin's late nudes. He drinks more. He is 42.

### 1928 — age 26–27 (Hemingway)

- **Brief affair with Hemingway.** Documented (Braude). A few weeks. A fight. A permanent distance.
- Hemingway writes the foreword to her memoir. Three drafts. *"The woman who has never been a lady, but a queen."*
- Hemingway publishes *A Farewell to Arms* (1929).
- **Man Ray's *L'Étoile de Mer.*** Kiki is the lead and **finally credited.** It is the year she begins leaving him.
- Edward Titus arranges to bring Samuel Putnam's English translation to the US.

### 1929 — age 27–28 (the crown, the break)

- Memoir published (Henri Broca, Paris). Hemingway foreword. *Souvenirs de Kiki.*
- **Henri Broca crowns her Queen of Montparnasse** at a cabaret. Paper crown. Cheap champagne.
- **The break with Man Ray.** He has been crediting her for one year. He understands too late. She leaves him for Broca.
- 24 October 1929 — Wall Street crash.

### 1930 — age 28–29 (Pascin)

- **2 June 1930: Pascin hangs himself** in his studio at 36 rue Joseph-Bara — two streets from where Mendjizky had been when he named her Kiki. The day before the opening of his show at the Galerie Bernheim-Jeune.
- US Customs seizes Samuel Putnam's English translation as obscene. No more English-language copies printed.

### 1931–1933 — age 29–32

- *Paris-Montparnasse* magazine continues, she with Henri Broca.
- The 1930s begin to feel different.
- Cocteau in deeper opium.
- Hemingway in Cuba on safari. *Death in the Afternoon* 1932. *Green Hills of Africa* 1935.
- Man Ray briefly with Lee Miller (until 1932). Then Adrienne Fidelin from 1936.

### 1934 — age 32

- **Henri Broca goes mad** the year she leaves him. *Why she leaves him is not in any source.* Committed to **Charenton asylum.**
- She is now living in a small apartment alone. Drinking has intensified.

### 1935 — age 33

- **23 August 1935: Henri Broca dies in Charenton.** *He was the most extreme outcome — could not bear losing her at all.*
- Mendjizky still alive, still painting, mostly unknown.

### 1936–1938 — age 34–37

- Kiki opens **Chez Kiki** on the rue Bréa — her own cabaret. Smaller crowd. Some old friends.
- Spanish Civil War. Pound moves to Italy permanently.
- Robert Desnos (Surrealist poet, born 1900) becomes a late lover. Documented in Anaïs Nin and others.
- Foujita returns to Japan in 1933.

### 1939 — age 38

- War.
- The cast scatters or stays. Man Ray will eventually flee to Hollywood (1940). Hemingway stays in Cuba and then becomes a war correspondent. Cocteau stays in Paris.
- Pound broadcasting from Rome.

### 1940–1944 — age 38–43 (Occupation)

- Paris under German occupation, June 1940 – August 1944.
- **Chez Kiki** stays open in some form. *What she did to survive is not in any source she or anyone else wrote down.* The book treats this with restraint and refuses to invent details.
- Many of the Jewish painters around her vanish. **Otto Freundlich** gassed at Lublin-Majdanek 1943. **Soutine** dies 9 Aug 1943 in the back of an ambulance trying to reach a hospital that wouldn't take him. Surgery in Paris for ulcer was the official cause. Kiki cannot get to the funeral. **Robert Desnos** is arrested February 1944, dies of typhus at Terezín three days after liberation (8 June 1945).
- Kiki drinks heavily through these years. Begins to lose teeth. Begins to lose the colour in her hair.

### 1945 — age 43 (Liberation)

- Paris liberated 25 August 1944. The Americans arrive.
- Desnos dies in June. She learns months later.

### 1946–1949 — age 44–47 (the wreckage)

- The Coupole becomes a tourist café. Le Sélect is in the Michelin.
- New cafés open with menus in English.
- Hemingway publishes *Across the River and Into the Trees* (1950). One million copies. He is 50 km away in a hotel in Paris occasionally. They do not see each other.
- Cocteau directs *La Belle et la Bête* (1946) and *Orphée* (1950).
- Foujita returns to France (1950), converts to Catholicism, eventually builds his chapel in Reims.

### 1950–1952 — age 48–50

- Kiki is in Montparnasse every night, in the same black dress, singing the same songs to rooms that get smaller.
- Drinking is now a condition.
- Friends raise small amounts for her.
- Berthe Weill dies 17 April 1951. **Mendjizky dies 14 September 1951.** No mention in any major paper.

### 1953 — age 51 (The end)

- Falls in the street in Montparnasse. Taken to **Pitié-Salpêtrière.**
- **29 April 1953: Kiki dies of cirrhosis at 51.** *The same day Moïse Kisling dies* (4 km away, lung complications, age 62). Two funerals in Montparnasse the same week.
- **Foujita comes from Tokyo for her funeral.** The collection for the coffin is small. *The book's emotional close.*

### After

- Foujita dies 29 Jan 1968. Buried at the chapel he built in Reims.
- Man Ray dies 18 Nov 1976 in Paris. Photographs of Kiki line the walls of his last studio.
- Hemingway publishes *A Moveable Feast* in 1964 (posthumous) — written 1956–1961 in his last years. One paragraph about her. *He could not bring himself to write more.*
- Aaron writes the single sentence about the apple in 1968. *(The piece of paper in Bill's parcel.)*
- Pound dies in Venice 1972, mostly silent the last decade.
- **2013** — the painting surfaces in Bratislava when Robert's cousin Vlado's daughter dies and the family takes it out from behind the curtain in the back room.
- **2022** — Richard meets Bill.

## Discipline rules for braiding the cast

When drafting any chapter, before writing:

1. **Check the timeline for the year the chapter is in.** Who is in the room?
2. **Pick at least three Lost Boys from the timeline for that year.** They must appear by name in the chapter — even if just a glance, a one-line aside, a passing reference to where they are in their own life that year. *No new chapter introduces a Lost Boy who has not been mentioned before.*
3. **Returning characters get returning details.** Pascin's brown coat. Soutine's wet face. Foujita's small black moustache. Mendjizky's grey suit Pascin gave him. *The reader knows them by the small repeated details before they die.*
4. **Deaths are foreshadowed by appearances.** Pascin must appear in Chapter II, Chapter VII, Chapter VIII before his suicide in Chapter IX. *The reader who has been with him for four chapters feels the suicide.*
5. **The same applies to Bill in the modern arc.** Bill must be present in Chapters I, II (postcard reference), III (Camille asks how he is), IV (death), V (zibaldone read on plane), VI (the file he was building), and into the chapter where Marc signs. *Bill stays in the book after he dies.*
